First Santa Cruz Date

📍 Natural Bridges State Beach
📅 11/7/2020

Our first date in Santa Cruz! I remember being so excited that Ariel was interested enough in me to come all the way to Santa Cruz, which I learned that she never visited because of the scary highway 17. I took her to Natural Bridges to see the monarch butterflies which normally would be migrating and overwintering here, but just my luck! That year was a historically poor year for the butterflies and hardly any were to be seen. All was not lost though. After walking through the trails we went to the lookout point and I spotted dolphins in the distance. I had my binos with me and quickly shared them with Ariel. I remembered her saying that she hadn't seen dolphins around before so I was ecstatic that I could help her spot some!

Mount Shasta Vacay 🏔️

📍 Mount Shasta
📅 12/28/2020

The first vacation we ever took together was at Mount Shasta. In the the middle of the pandemic, Shasta County was the only one in the state without travel restrictions so that's where we decided to go 😆. We stayed at nice AirBnB that was right in the backyard of Mount Shasta. You could literally see it through the window! Ariel took me cross-country skiing ⛷️ for the first time! I really had no idea what I was getting into but I trusted her that it would be a good time, and it was. We lucked out on some spectacular weather, and there was hardly anyone around. It felt like we had the whole mountain to ourselves. We somehow ended up on a black diamond slope and I recall eating it a couple times, but boy what a thrill.

Tom's Dirty 3️0️ pt. 1

📍 Morro Bay
📅 5/29/2021

For my big 3 0, we went on another vacay this time down south. Solvang was this Danish styled town both in architecture and food, where we enjoyed beers and marveled in this adult Disneyland. However, the highlight of the trip was the Channel Islands! It took a about 1.5 hours by ferry to get to this idyllic island off the coast of Santa Barbara. Santa Cruz island to be exact. What a beautiful island, with stunning views along the trails, and my absolute favorite part of the trip - seeing the endemic and super cute island fox! The little guys were the size of cats and very curious.

Mexico 🇲🇽 pt. 1

📍 Downtown
📅 6/26/2021

Ariel and I's first international trip together was all the way in Mexico 🇲🇽! We went back and forth about where we wanted to go but ultimately decided on Mexico City where we wanted to have a grand food tour 🍴. We stayed at several locations along the time, but the first was the Downtown Hotel. It was easily the most luxurious hotel I had ever stayed at, and will always remember. Boy, looking back we did a ton of walking and got to see so many parts of the city. We got to see the landmarks of centro histórico, the hip neighborhoods of Roma and Condesa, and the cobbled streets of Coyoacán. From street food to fine dining, cochinita pibil to Vietnamese tamarind prawns, CDMX showed us what a vibrant city it is.
